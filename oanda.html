Regulation

OANDA is a highly regulated broker, operating under strict regulatory standards across several major financial jurisdictions. This ensures a secure and transparent trading environment for its users around the world.

  • National Futures Association (NFA) - USA
  • Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) - UK
  • Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) - Australia
  • Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) - Singapore

All US regulated forex brokers are subject to non-segregated accounts, FIFO (first in first out) and non-hedging. Oanda handles the FIFO rule implementation differently than other US brokers, which is very positive in our view. The FIFO rule prevents orders of the same lot size that belong to the same currency pair to be closed out of order. Oanda’s solution to this is to simply prevent orders of the same exact lot size on the same currency pair, which allows orders or varied lot sizes to be closed in any order. This is a convenient implementation that allows traders to manage trades with less friction while still abiding by the FIFO rules.
